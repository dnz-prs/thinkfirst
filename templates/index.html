Why does it work this way?
Cognitive science has a concept called the generation effect: information you produce yourself — through effort, guessing, and reasoning — is retained far more strongly than information you passively receive.

When an AI just hands you an answer, your brain barely processes it. When you have to reason towards an answer, you actually know it afterward — not just recognise it.

So every time ThinkFirst asks you a question instead of answering yours, it's not being difficult. It's making the learning stick.
